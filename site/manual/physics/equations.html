PDE Systems

Radial stellar pulsations and stellar pulsations in general are described by hydrodynamics. The system of equations are consisting of the conservation of mass, the conservation of momentum and conservation of energy. Although stars are made up plasma, the magnetic field and rotation is usually neglected in these models, due to the complexity of the problem.

Thus in vector formulation our governing equations are

(1)\[\begin{split}\frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} + \boldsymbol \nabla \cdot (\rho \boldsymbol v) &= 0 \\ \frac{\partial \boldsymbol v}{\partial t} + \boldsymbol v \cdot \boldsymbol \nabla \boldsymbol v &= -\frac{1}{\rho} \boldsymbol \nabla p - \frac{1}{\rho} \boldsymbol \nabla \cdot \boldsymbol{\underline \tau} - \boldsymbol g \\ \frac{\partial }{\partial t}\left(e + \frac 1 2 \vert\boldsymbol v \vert^2\right) + \boldsymbol v \cdot \boldsymbol \nabla \left(e + \frac 1 2 \vert\boldsymbol v \vert^2\right) &= -\frac{1}{\rho} \boldsymbol \nabla \cdot \left( p \boldsymbol v + \boldsymbol F_{\text rad} + \boldsymbol{\underline \tau} \cdot \boldsymbol v \right) - \boldsymbol g \cdot \boldsymbol v\end{split}\]

Where \(\rho\) is the density, \(\boldsymbol v\) is the velocity, \(\boldsymbol g\) is graviational acceleration, \(p\) denotes pressure, \(\boldsymbol{\underline \tau}\) is the viscous stress tensor, \(e\) denotes specific internal energy and \(F_{\text rad}\) means radiative flux (or generally a heat transfer).

Converting these equations into conservation form, we arrive at a partial differential equation systems, with the generic form:

(2)\[\frac{\partial q}{\partial t} + \boldsymbol \nabla \cdot \boldsymbol F_{\text adv}(q) + \boldsymbol \nabla \cdot\boldsymbol F_{\text diff}(q) = S (q)\]

In this generic form, one can define the so called state-vector as \(\boldsymbol q = (\rho, \rho u , \rho v, \rho w, \rho e_{\rm tot} )\).

Implementation

Since the state-vector couples all information that is available in a grid point, it should be implemented together. Therefore, we implement it as an array-based templated format, called QuantityBase:

These are implemented through the various classes (see File equations.hpp header documentation).
